Scholarships secretariat engages GRASAG on graduate research funding

Published: 28 October, 2019

The Ghana Scholarships Secretariat has met with the leadership of the Graduate Students Association of Ghana (GRASAG) to discuss issues of research funding and how best a sustainable means of support can be made available to the Association's members. The meeting which took place last week is part of broad stakeholders’ consultation the secretariat has started embarking on in addressing problems associated with research funding in the country.

The cost of graduate research keeps increasing by the day. As it stands now, regular postgraduate students in the various public universities in the country get some cushioning from the scholarship secretariat in a form of Graduate Thesis and Bursary grant.

The amount paid to the students ranges between GH350 to GHC1000 depending on the program and level of the student. This has however proven to be woefully inadequate since according to GRASAG the cheapest form of research in this country cost over GHC3000 with some costing several thousands of Ghana Cedis.

Grant for Bursary

Speaking at the meeting, the registrar of the secretariat, Mr. Kingsley Agyeman stated that due to the high cost of research especially by students offering Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) courses, the secretariat is considering cancelling the Bursary Grant. In this case he said funding can be channeled into a single pool out of which  relevant research that solves pressing issues in the country can have better funding from the secretariat. This will ensure that useful research gets adequate funding rather than the current ‘free for all’ system where every researcher gets ‘something small’.

However Mr. Agyeman said the secretariat is not considering cancelling the bursary now because consultation on the proposal has now begun. He justified the importance of cancelling the bursaries and focusing more on strengthening and improving on the funding for STEM courses saying this is  now the global practice.

Objection

However, GRASAG members objected to the cancellation of the bursary. They suggested to the secretariat to look for alternative sources of funding to increase the amount paid to the students and to also have extra support means for the STEM and other equally relevant researches.  GRASAG proffered other alternatives sources of funding for the secretariat and urged them to consider them.

Collaboration

Mr. Agyeman assured GRASAG that the secretariat will consider all views expressed. He also urged GRASAG to consider the proposal from the secretariat and come back for further discussion. He further called for extensive collaboration between the Association and the scholarships secretariat. He also advised GRASAG to get a national secretariat, independent of the tenure of office of the elected leaders of GRASAG. This, he said will help the association to position itself well to welcome existing opportunities.

The meeting was attended by the national leadership of GRASAG, institutional presidents of the various universities, some institutional executives of GRASAG as well as management of the scholarship secretariat.
